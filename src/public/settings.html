Voice Activity Detection (VAD)

Controls how the always-on listener detects and filters speech from background noise. Lower values = more sensitive, higher values = stricter filtering.

WebRTC is lightweight and fast. Silero uses a neural network for higher accuracy and outputs a confidence score (0-1) instead of binary speech/no-speech.
Sensitive Strict 0.02
Pre-filter: audio chunks quieter than this RMS level are instantly discarded before any speech analysis. Eliminates fan hum, mic self-noise, and quiet background sounds. Raise this if you get false triggers in a quiet room.
Loose Strict 0.70
What percentage of 30ms frames in a 100ms chunk must be classified as speech by WebRTC VAD. At 0.7, 70% of frames need to contain speech. Higher values mean more of the chunk must be actual speech — reduces false positives from keyboard clicks or coughs.
Sensitive Strict 0.50
Silero model confidence threshold (0-1). Lower = more sensitive to faint speech, higher = stricter filtering. 0.5 is a balanced default.
50 500 250
Minimum speech segment duration (in milliseconds) before Silero considers it real speech.
50 500 100
Minimum silence duration (in milliseconds) to end a speech segment in Silero's internal state.
Short Long 0.75
Minimum duration of detected speech before it's sent to Whisper for transcription. Anything shorter is discarded as noise. Prevents short bursts (door slam, cough) from triggering transcription.
Quick Patient 1.00
How many seconds of silence after speech before the utterance is considered complete. Lower = faster response but may split sentences. Higher = waits longer for the speaker to continue, better for slow speakers or pauses mid-thought.
Short Long 30
Force-flush the audio buffer after this many seconds of continuous speech, even if the speaker hasn't paused. Prevents unbounded memory usage during long monologues. The flushed audio is transcribed and a new buffer starts.
1 6 3
After Whisper transcribes the audio, discard results with fewer words than this. Filters out hallucinated short phrases like "Thank you" or "Bye" that Whisper produces from noise. Increase if you still see gibberish one-liners.
